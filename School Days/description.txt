School Days - a co-op visual novel you play by voting.

One school year, told from a single point of view - but every decision is made
TOGETHER. Whenever a choice appears, the whole lobby votes; the option with the
most votes wins (a 60-second timer keeps things moving, and some quiet moments
have no clock at all). Between choices the story just flows - anyone can press
Continue when everyone's done reading.

Live your last year at Pinar High: partner up for the big project, fall for the
quiet artist Aylin, keep up with your loud best friend Kerem, stay on the right
side of Ms. Deniz - and decide what you do when an exam-answer scandal tears the
class apart. Kindness, ambition, courage and cowardice all quietly add up: the
year branches through four chapters into SIX different endings, from a golden
romance to a lonely honour roll to a long, guilty shadow.

This is a reference example for the Visual Novel runtime. The whole story -
characters, dialog, branches, hidden choices, variables and every ending - lives
in story/school_days.json, the exact format the Online Asset Editor's Visual
Novel tab exports. All the presentation (background, character sprite, name box,
dialog, the voting HUD) is ordinary Lua you can read in general/scripts, so you
can see precisely how to turn an exported story into a playable game.

The art in general/images is flat single-colour PLACEHOLDER template - one solid
tint per background and per character mood (neutral / happy / sad / angry / ...).
Repaint them freely; the story only ever references them by name, so your art
drops straight in. Build your own story in the editor, export the JSON, point
STORY_PATH at it, and you have a brand-new branching game.
